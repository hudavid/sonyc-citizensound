SONYC: Tutorial

Welcome to New York University's sound tagging web-application. Please read this tutorial carefully before proceeding.

Upon completing this tutorial you will be presented with a form. You must fill out this form before proceeding.

Filling out the form:
At the top of the form page you will find a personal code. Please copy and paste this code into the first box of the form:

After pasting your personal code, please complete the remainder of the form and click the "submit" button:

Once you've submitted the form, a "Continue" button will appear to proceed to the application.

Your goal:
In this web-app, we ask you to tag (i.e. to label) the different sources of sound that you can hear in a sound clip. Examples of sound sources are "car horn", "dog bark", "siren", etc. Your goal is to label all the sounds you can hear in the provided sound clip.

Listening to the sound clip:
We will present you with a sound clip, visualized as a spectrogram. Beneath the spectrogram you will find the Play and Rewind buttons that allow you to listen to the sound as many times as you like. There is also a Show Tutorial button that allows you to go back to this tutorial at any time:

Tagging sound sources: Step 1
After listening to the sound clip, the next step is to tag all the sound sources you've heard. For example, let's say you heard a car horn. We start by writing the source name in the provided textbox:

Next, you are required to select a salience level, i.e. is the car horn in the foreground or background? If you're not sure, you can select not sure:

Finally click the + button to add the tag:

Tagging sound sources: Steps 1 (continued)

Using auto-complete: when you start writing the name of a sound source, an auto-complete list will pop-up with suggestions:

If the sound source you heard is included in the list, please use the provided suggestion rather than typing it out yourself. If the sound source is not included in the list please type it out.

Tagging sound sources: Step 2
The tag you added will now appear in the added tags list, under the salience level you selected:

If there is more than one sound source in the recording, you should add a tag for every source:

If you add a tag by mistake, you can delete it by clicking on it in the tag list:

Tagging sound sources: Step 3
Once you've finished tagging all the sound sources in the recording, click submit to save your tags and move on to the next sound:
